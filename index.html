When I first started teaching myself programming, my first foray was Andrew Ng'sMachine Learning course, and I gave up only two or so weeks in to the course. It wasn't until much later that I discovered that machine learning is a topic generally not broached until graduate school -- I hadn't taken the 'prerequisites.' Chancel.es will allow the curation of 'courses of study' that recommend resources to study, and an order to study them in.

There are lots of places to go for free knowledge on the internet -- Coursera, Udacity, Khan Academy, Youtube, Wikipedia and dozens of others, but if you try to learn multiplication from Wikipedia, you're gonna have a bad time. So, where do you start? What do you do next? Chancel.es defines courses of action for mastering new skills, like a registrar at a University.

FAQ

Will completing a course of study through Chancel.es be the equiavent to a University degree?
No. Chancel.es facilitates learning, but is not an accredited education provider. Chancel.es is simply an aggregator of educational resources; the benefit comes from the knowledge you gain, not from completing a Chancel.es course of study.
Is Chancel.es just for MOOCs?
No! Chancel.es will be more flexible than MOOCs can provide on their own. Courses of study can be in any field -- a course of articles to familiarize learners with a current event (for instance, the situation in Eygpt); a course of books, exercises and projects for a programming language, a course of YouTube videos for painting.
Why are you raising funds?
I've got a day job. I spend evenings working on the project, but if I could spend a few months working on Chancel.es exclusively, it'd get off the ground a lot sooner.
